Quick help center

What is the goal of this site?

Give players an easy way to trade items via an organized marketplace. Let spamming in Kamadan be a thing of the past.

What do I need to do to get an item?

Type the name of the item in the search bar or browse the recent orders. Once you have found one with the right price, you can click the chat icon to copy & paste a message to the vendor in-game. Alternatively you can suggest meetings times and more (see section Messages & Interactions)

How do I set up my shop? How do I post orders?

No log-in or account is required. No data can be stolen. Simply create an item order, set your character name, and update your shop. The data is stored in the browser's cache, but you are recommended to save it to file, or bind it to a character via the certification process.

How do I find the one thing I am really looking for?

You can use the filter icon right next to the search. Select the type and category and even specific modifiers, filter whatever your heart desires.

What does being highlighted mean for my shop?

Currently, it is an arbitrary 15 min timer during which your shop items are promoted the most (recent section). You can enable notifications to alert you, when your shop is expiring. You can also tell by the decreasing level within the power button icon on the top right of your market page. Your last updated shop time will be shown to players looking for items. Don't worry, your items will stay visible for one week, and are saved in your shop until you delete them.

How do I get the blue certified badge?

The blue badge is a feature linked to the player certification, see more in Certification & Accounts section.

Why is my shop empty?

If your shop appears empty, it is usually because your browser cache was cleared. Your shop data is stored in your browser cache, not on the website. To avoid losing it, regularly export your data so you can reimport your listings at any time. You can also bring your shop data back by certifying a character you already certified before.

Reputation system

Once certified, you may collect and give reputation votes. You can do this by clicking the thumbs up and down on other player's profiles. You may only use it once per account – Doing it again will result in the removal of our previous upvote. You will also be notified within your messages about received votes and can return the favour by clicking the arrow leading to their shop next to their name. Down votes require a provided reason. We also encourage you to screenshot unusual interactions to discourage bad behaviour.

What does certifying a character do?

To certify your character you may send an in-game message to a verification account. All it does is let the owner of the website know, that the character you registered actually exists. It is a scam protection. It is a recommended step also to help recover your shop if you clean your cache or use another device.

My character is already certified on another shop, what do I do?

You need to certify the same character once more, this will link your old shop to the new one and merge the content of both shops. If you use 2 devices, they should now be synced.

Why is there no option to delete a character?

In order to make sure that people deliver upon their offers and auctions, we need to ensure a level of accountability. This is for the protection of all other players. Hence, we also recommend you do not use a PVP char for verification.

How do I swap in between registered characters/accounts?

By pressing player on the left you can open the certification menu and swap in between. While using Daybreak this change is automatic. You can also do multiple accounts (not recommended with Daybreak however. See details in other section.)

Can I use multiple accounts?

Yes, if you are entering your items manually, there should be no obstacles. However, should you decide to use Daybreak this might come with limitations. While you can run multiple clients, only one account is reliably tracked for online status (usually the first one launched). For the best experience, use one account for trading and avoid switching between clients mid-session.

How do I contact a player?

Use the search bar or filters to find the item you want, then chose a listing. Next to the player's name, you will see a chat icon and a letter icon.

Click the chat icon to open a window with a pre-typed in-game whisper, which you can copy and send to the online player in Guild Wars. The letter icon opens the full interaction menu, where you can view and manage requests such as time slot, time window, or negotiate.

What are "time slot", "time window", and "negotiate" requests?

These are the different ways a player can interact with your listing:

Time slot – a specific time is proposed for the trade
Time window – a flexible time range is proposed
Negotiate – gives the opportunity to propose a counter offer

The letter icon opens the full menu with the request details.

How does the notifications panel work?

This panel shows all interactions with your shop. Since there is no open chat on the site, notifications such as time slot, time window, or negotiate requests will appear here. Use the provided character name to contact the player in-game and complete the trade.

After a completed trade, you may also receive reputation notifications, allowing both buyer and seller to leave feedback for each other.

How do I reply to requests?

The notifications now come with the option to reply. You may accept the proposed time or offer. You can also decline or make other proposals. Please use the provided templates. Free text on the website can not be implemented as it would require legal supervision. You are free to contact people in-game with whatever message you deem suitable.

How do I mark a notification as read?

By clicking once on the left highlighted part of the message or on the message itself you can mark it as read. This depends on the browser you use.

How are time slots handled across time zones?

Your time zone is automatically determined by your browser/operating system. When you propose a time (e.g. 18:30), it is shown in your local time and automatically converted to the other player's local time on their side.

How do I set up Daybreak?

Daybreak is a custom launcher and open the API in the options. This lets the web-browser chat with your game. Here is the Github page where you can download it:
https://github.com/gwdevhub/Daybreak
First enable Daybreak API in the mods manager, then launch Guild Wars through Daybreak , make sure localhost (127.0.0.1) is not blocked by browser extensions, and open the account you want tracked first.

Why am I shown offline or on the wrong account? Does Daybreak support multiple accounts?

This is usually caused by blocked localhost, launching the wrong client first, or a delayed refresh. GWMarket typically reads a single first active client / port such as 5080 –5100, so if the wrong account is tracked, close all clients and launch the correct one first. Multi-accounting with daybreak is challenging. For the smoothest setup, use one account for trading and avoid switching characters mid-session.

Why is my status delayed or not updating instantly?

Status checks are not instant, so online or offline updates can take up to one minute to appear. The market tries for a maximum of 3 minutes before the shop is shown offline. To relaunch the process, refresh the page with F5.

Why did Daybreak stop working in PvP or after switching characters?

PvP restrictions are intended behaviour and not a bug. Character switching and some launch parameters may also be inconsistent depending on setup. If tracking breaks, relaunch the client you want tracked first and refresh the page.
